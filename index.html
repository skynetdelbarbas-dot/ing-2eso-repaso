📖 Fundamentos — lo que hay que saberse de memoria

Verb TO BE

Presente: I am · You/We/They are · He/She/It is

Pasado: I/He/She/It was · You/We/They were

Participio: been (been → sido/estado)

Contracciones: I'm, you're, he's, she's, it's, we're, they're

I am a student.
Soy estudiante.

Verb TO HAVE

Presente: I/You/We/They have · He/She/It has

Pasado: had (para todos)

Participio: had

Have got = tener (más común en UK): I've got, he's got

She has a car. / She has got a car.
Ella tiene un coche.

Verb TO GET

Presente: I/You/We/They get · He/She/It gets

Pasado: got

Participio: got (UK) / gotten (US)

Usos: conseguir, recibir, llegar, volverse (get tired), comprar (get milk)

3ª persona del singular -s

En present simple, he/she/it añade -s o -es:

• Normal: work → works, play → plays

• -s, -ss, -sh, -ch, -x, -o → +es: watch → watches, go → goes

• Consonante + y → ies: study → studies, cry → cries

• Have → has (única irregular)

Reglas esenciales

• DO/DOES para negativas e interrogativas en presente

• DID para negativas e interrogativas en pasado

• Jamás doble negación: I don't have nothing → I don't have anything

• Adjetivos antes del sustantivo: a red car (no "a car red")

• Plurales: la mayoría +s; -s,-ss,-sh,-ch,-x,-o +es; -y → -ies

🗣️ Modal Verbs

Los modales no llevan -s en 3ª persona, no necesitan do/does, y van seguidos de infinitivo sin to.

CAN / COULD

can = poder (habilidad, permiso) · could = podía/podría (pasado, condicional)

can't = no poder · couldn't = no podía

I can swim. / Could you help me?

MAY / MIGHT

may = puede que (más probable) · might = podría ser que (menos probable)

It may rain. / It might snow.

MUST / HAVE TO

must = deber (obligación personal, opinión del hablante)

have to = tener que (obligación externa, reglas)

mustn't = prohibición · don't have to = no es necesario

You must study. / You have to wear a uniform.

SHOULD / OUGHT TO

should = debería (consejo) · ought to = debería (más formal)

You should rest. / You ought to apologize.

HAD BETTER

had better ('d better) = más vale que (consecuencia negativa si no se hace)

You'd better hurry or we'll miss the train.

WILL / WOULD

will = futuro, promesas, decisiones · would = condicional, cortesía

I will call you. / Would you like tea?

🗣️ Future Tenses

WILL

will + infinitivo → decisiones espontáneas, predicciones, promesas

I will help you. / It will rain tomorrow.

GOING TO

am/is/are + going to + infinitivo → planes, intenciones, evidencia presente

I'm going to study tonight. / Look! It's going to rain.

PRESENT CONTINUOUS

am/is/are + -ing → futuro con planes ya organizados (hora/lugar fijado)

I'm meeting Sarah at 6pm.

PRESENT SIMPLE

→ futuro con horarios fijos (trenes, vuelos, clases)

The train leaves at 8am.

🗣️ Comparatives & Superlatives

Adjetivos cortos (1 sílaba)

Comparativo: adj + -er + than · Superlativo: the + adj + -est

big → bigger → the biggest · hot → hotter → the hottest · easy → easier → the easiest

Adjetivos largos (2+ sílabas)

Comparativo: more + adj + than · Superlativo: the most + adj

beautiful → more beautiful → the most beautiful

Irregulares

good → better → the best

bad → worse → the worst

far → farther/further → the farthest/furthest

Igualdad

as + adj + as = tan ... como

not as + adj + as = no tan ... como

She is as tall as her brother. / This isn't as easy as I thought.

🗣️ Just / Already / Yet / Still / For / Since

JUST — acabar de

Con Present Perfect: acción muy reciente. have/has + just + participio

I've just finished my homework.
Acabo de terminar los deberes.

ALREADY — ya (afirmativas)

Con Present Perfect: algo ha ocurrido antes de lo esperado

She's already left.
Ya se ha ido.

YET — aún/todavía (negativas), ya (interrogativas)

Con Present Perfect, al final de la frase

I haven't finished yet. / Have you eaten yet?

STILL — todavía/aún

Algo que continúa. Va antes del verbo principal o después de be

I'm still waiting. / She still hasn't called.

FOR — durante (período)

Responde a ¿cuánto tiempo? → for two hours, for a week, for ages

SINCE — desde (punto de inicio)

Responde a ¿desde cuándo? → since Monday, since 2020, since I was a child

I've lived here for 5 years since 2021.

✍️ Writing — Plantillas

📧 Formal Email

Estructura

Subject: [Asunto claro y breve] Dear Mr/Ms [Surname], I am writing to [motivo]. [Párrafo 1: introducción y contexto] [Párrafo 2: detalles, información adicional] I look forward to hearing from you. Yours sincerely, [Tu nombre completo]

📧 Informal Email

Estructura

Subject: [Asunto informal] Hi [Name], How are you? I hope you're doing well. I'm writing to [motivo]. [Párrafo 1: noticias, lo que has hecho] [Párrafo 2: planes, preguntas, lo que quieres contar] Write back soon! Love / Best wishes / Take care, [Tu nombre]

📰 Article / Blog Post

Estructura

TITLE: [Atractivo, con pregunta o dato curioso] Introduction: Hook the reader with a question or interesting fact. Say what the article is about. Body paragraph 1: First point or argument with examples. Body paragraph 2: Second point or contrasting view. Include personal experiences if relevant. Conclusion: Summarize and give your final opinion. End with a question for the reader to think about.

📖 Story / Narrative

Estructura

Opening: Set the scene — who, where, when. Create atmosphere. "It was a dark and stormy night..." Build-up: Introduce the problem or conflict. Describe feelings and reactions. Climax: The most exciting/dramatic moment. Use short sentences for tension. Resolution: How it ended. What happened next. Tie up loose ends. Time expressions: Suddenly, then, later, after a while, in the end, eventually, meanwhile...

⭐ Review

Estructura

Introduction: What are you reviewing? (film, book, restaurant, product). Give basic facts (name, author, price, etc.) Paragraph 1: Positive aspects. What you liked and why. Give specific examples. Paragraph 2: Negative aspects (be diplomatic). What could be improved. Conclusion: Overall opinion. Star rating or recommendation. Would you recommend it? To whom?
