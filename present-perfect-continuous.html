⏳ Present Perfect Continuous

Aprende a usar el Present Perfect Continuous para acciones que empezaron en el pasado y continúan o tienen efectos visibles ahora (nivel B1).

📖 Teoría rápida — Present Perfect Continuous

🔧 Estructura

Afirmativa: have / has + been + verb-ing
I have been waiting for an hour.
She has been studying all morning.

Negativa: haven't / hasn't been + verb-ing
We haven't been sleeping well lately.

Interrogativa: Have / Has + subject + been + verb-ing?
Have you been working out?

🎯 ¿Cuándo lo usamos?

1. Duración de una acción que continúa:
I have been studying English for three years. (sigo estudiando)

2. Acción recién terminada con resultados visibles:
Your eyes are red — Have you been crying?

3. Énfasis en la duración o el esfuerzo:
I have been trying to call you all day!

⏱️ FOR vs SINCE

FOR = duración (cuánto tiempo)
for two hours, for a week, for ages

SINCE = punto de inicio (desde cuándo)
since Monday, since 2019, since I was a child

⚖️ Present Perfect Simple vs Continuous

Simple → resultado / acción completada:
I have written three emails. (el resultado: 3 emails escritos)

Continuous → duración / actividad en desarrollo:
I have been writing emails all morning. (el esfuerzo: toda la mañana escribiendo)

💡 Regla clave: Con stative verbs (know, be, like, believe, understand...), usa siempre el Simple:
I have known him for years. ✅ (No: ❌ I have been knowing)
She has been studying for three hours. 📚
They have been waiting since 8 o'clock. ⏰
I have been feeling tired lately. 😴
