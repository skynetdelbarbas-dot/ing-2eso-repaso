Have Got

Aprende a usar have got para hablar de posesión y relaciones familiares (nivel A1).

📖 Teoría rápida — Have Got

✚ Afirmativa

I have got = I've got
You have got = You've got
He / She / It has got = He's / She's / It's got
We have got = We've got
They have got = They've got

I've got a new phone.
She's got a beautiful house.

✚ Negativa

I haven't got (have not got)
You haven't got
He / She / It hasn't got (has not got)
We haven't got
They haven't got

I haven't got a car.
She hasn't got any brothers.

❓ Interrogativa

Have I got...?
Have you got...?
Has he / she / it got...?
Have we got...?
Have they got...?

Have you got a pet?
Has she got a sister?

📌 Usos principales

Posesión: I've got a new bike.
Relaciones familiares: He's got two brothers.
Características físicas: She's got blue eyes.
Enfermedades / estados: I've got a headache.
She's got a dog and two cats. 🐕🐱
They haven't got a garden. 🌿
Have you got a pen I can borrow? ✏️
